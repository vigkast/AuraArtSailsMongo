Artist Shailesh Patel in conversation with Dr S K Kushwaha, Dean & Head of the Art History and Aesthetics Department, Faculty of Fine Arts, M S University of Baroda


Dr S K Kushwaha: You are popular as an artist. What differentiates an artist from other people?
Shailesh Patel: If I am a known painter today, it has only been after years of struggIe. In my view, an artist is as much a part of society as anyone else, but he differs from the common man in terms of his sensitivity, the ability to visualise and the skills he is endowed with.

Dr S K Kushwaha:. At what age did you decide to pursue art? Was there any inspiration?
Shailesh Patel: After completing my schooling, I joined the Faculty of Fine Arts, M S University of Baroda at the age of eighteen. I did my B A Fine Arts in painting which I finished in 1986 -- thereafter Post-Diploma in Mural and finally, M A in Creative Painting in 1993.
During my childhood days, I was always attracted towards the visual aspects in and around, manifested in different shades of colours and the impact it had on human life. That served as a great source of inspiration during my study and training which was more of an institutionalised mode of art education.

Dr S K Kushwaha: I could see the stress you made on the "insitutionalised mode of art education. " Did you, at any point of time, think that the course was too tough for you to continue?
Shailesh Patel: On the contrary. Whatever challenges I had faced during my formal education in art, motivated me all the more and proved to be more rewarding. I never thought of them as tasks and that boosted my self-confidence. It also helped me rely on my tremendous energy. I persisted and it paid off. Never did I think even once to escape from those hardships during my years of study. I learnt a great deal in terms of techniques, perception and evolved a highly personalized visual vocabulary during my eight painstaking years of devotion as a student of art.

Dr S K Kushwaha: As a professor of art, I am happy to hear that. But tell me, can you trace back any significant links with art during your early schooldays?
Shailesh Patel: During my schooldays, my drawing teacher used to drag us in for art lessons. To tell you the truth, it was very monotonous and I almost developed an apathy towards art education. It troubled me to think that I should be in search of other sources to develop my dormant talent in art. But once I got an appropriate avenue in terms of college education, I was happy with myself and developed my own expression. Therefore, I strongly feel the need to inspire art-minded children, guide them in the right direction and develop their sensibilities.

Dr S K Kushwaha: What was the style and subject you first dealt with while creating artworks? Was there an ideology behind your work?
Shailesh Patel: My form and contents in painting revolves around different human conditions under various socio-cultural situations. They are essentially narratives in visual framework, relying on the linear quality of line with pigmentation underneath. I strongly believe in humanity and try to convey through representative figures, man and his world around. This is pivotal in my paintings, but it keeps on mutating.

Dr S K Kushwaha: You said your portrayal was of man and his world around. Is there any particular aspect of his world you normally depict?
Shailesh Patel: My imagery's stem usually from the mundane life of man and his surroundings. I use different techniques in my work based on the monumental and the diagonal layering of multiple perspectives in close- knit interiors and demystification of women's faces with a subtle eroticism. Of late, I am exploring mechanical human faces in my compositions.


Dr S K Kushwaha: That's quite creditable. With the passing of time, have there been any changes in your creative approach?
Shailesh Patel: Yes, quite often my style undergoes rampant transformation but never failing to maintain a sense of continuity though. Therefore, there are stylistic variations which can be noticed from time to time but they are nevertheless coherent to the scheme of my overall work plan.

Dr S K Kushwaha: Did you have a mentor who inspired you?
Shailesh Patel: Initially I was inspired by Gulam Mohammed Sheikh's work and he had helped me tremendously during my early days in the faculty. Certainly I owe much to the great teachers who taught me in the studio and many of them are living stalwarts in the contemporary art scene. Whatever I have gained, I owe it to my mentors who used to correct me every time I lost my sense of direction. Quite some time later , I chanced to meet Mr Harmeet Singh Sethi, CEO of Aura Art, quite a unique organisation in the art world.... set up to identify artists of calibre and encourage them besides promoting well-known artists of the day. The ideology of Aura Art, their qualitative inputs and the inspiration they provided, contributed immensely in shaping my growth as an artist.


Dr S K Kushwaha: That indeed is a happy situation and your family must be happy too. Tell me, how much support did you get from your family and from whom in particular ?
Shailesh Patel: I belong to a humble background, son of parents who were both school teachers. They were my sole supporters in my early days as a painter and when I decided to start my career as a freelancer. I could have managed a job but my heart never allowed me to pursue something like that. So, you see, I had to face tremendous hardships during the early days of my career. My father was an epitome of sacrifice in shaping my career as a professional painter. Without his moral, financial and encouraging support I could not have reached this stage of success.

Dr S K Kushwaha: How would you rate yourself as an artist vis-Ã -vis other artists? How are you different?
Shailesh Patel: This is a very tricky question you are asking to me! Every artist has his own rating; it all depends on the consistency and sustainability in his time of boom along with other market forces. One point is clear -- the pricing and saleability of an artwork cannot be the sole criteria to assess anybody's ratings. But I feel confident, the day is not far when I will be counted among the top artists of our times -- and I do not hesitate in saying, I will be at par with M F Hussain and Pablo Picasso's stature.

Dr S K Kushwaha: You have shifted to a new larger house at Vadodara. Has it influenced your work in any way?
Shailesh Patel: Yes, I shifted to my new large house some time back with the help of friends and well-wishers. It has provided me with good working space to paint without interruption. The serene environment has influenced my creative space as well, allowing me to concentrate and contemplate.

Dr S K Kushwaha: What does the future hold for you? Do you believe you will make a mark on the art scene of India?
Shailesh Patel: I am very optimistic about my future and can prove my worth as a talented artist. I am confident I will make a mark not only on the post modern art scene of India but that of the world too!

Dr S K Kushwaha: So that means the value of your work is on the upswing. What is your latest "scoreâ?
Shailesh Patel: Between four lacs to five lacs rupees at the Osian auction of 2008.

Dr S K Kushwaha: Having been an aspiring, budding artist yourself at one time, have you attempted in helping other budding artists?
Shailesh Patel: Yes, I always make it a point to introduce upcoming artists whom I consider worth interacting with, to buyers and collectors of art.

Dr S K Kushwaha: Do you believe that God is the creator of all beauty including art, and that the artist should first try to attain connectivity with the Master and then proceed to paint?
Shailesh Patel: Yes, I strongly believe God has created everything on this earth whether they are ugly or beautiful, animated or non animated. But the creation of art is a process which has to be monitored by human hands. That is why in oriental thought, imitation of nature was never accepted because nature's creator is different and nature cannot be imitated or interfered with. Whenever man has sought to disturb nature's equilibrium in any manner, it has only resulted in chaos and disaster.

Dr S K Kushwaha: Do you eventually intend ploughing back a good part of your earnings for the upliftment of art ?
Shailesh Patel: Yes, I certainly have some good social causes in mind. My long cherished ambition to help the blind and the preservation of nature that I owe a great deal to, are foremost in my mind. I know it may be a marathon task -- but to fulfill this wish, my wife and I, with her consent, decided not to raise children. The sacrifice she has made to see me succeed in life is monumental and cannot be compensated in material terms. Yes, I am very serious about the promotional aspects of art with the cooperation of art lovers and connoisseurs. I am sure I will be active in this area of activity too.
