    • In Pursuit of Balance

    H C Parikh

    Never in the history of mankind, the organic world has faced such an unprecedented crisis of existence. Our great forests are dying from acid rain, pollution or timber harvesting. Great rivers, all over, are in the process of decline from toxic wastes. The extinction rate amongst higher vertebrates is hundreds of times more than the average rate that prevailed over the history of evolution, seriously endangering the very survival of rare species, whether plants or animals, threatening to disturb the very Balance of Nature.

    No nation, society or group of people have any right to diminish or devalue the diversity, complexity or richness of nature's order or beauty of Mother Earth, but, alas, man in the name of growth and development has caused irreparable damage to the lovely Order of Nature, degrading the rich forests, cutting beautiful trees, polluting rivers and contaminating natural sources of water.


    # 507, My Countryside, Acrylic on canvas, 60 x 84 inches, 2008

    Artist's Thoughts
    Childhood memories are nestled in and around my village, on the banks of the river which was really exotic to view. Though life was a bit hard, yet, I enjoyed the darkness sitting near the chulhas at dusk for dinner. A tasty supper cooked on logs and sticks, listening to the unforgettable stories of my forefathers was really enchanting .. the look of flowering fields, with a few mango trees on the corners was heart-warming and indescribable. The scene is different now, I bemoan the prospering of villages at the cost of traditional ethos of cooperation, mutual care and tolerance.


    Nature is a masterly creative work of the almighty. It is a ceaseless process going on for millions and millions of years. It is an infinitely inventive phenomenon of nature, which unfolds before us the grandest beauty of a most comprehensive order, but man, pursuing the goal of material well being, has disturbed the balance and in the name of industrial revolution, has done great disservice to ecological aesthetics, hurting the environment to an extent that now man finds himself caught in a dilemma as to what he has lost for some tangible material gains.


    # 532, How Far I Am? Acrylic and oil on canvas, 84 x 120 inches, (Diptich), 2009

    Artist's Thoughts
    'How far I am?'' is an artwork that reflects my journey through life. I cast a little boy sitting at the top of a ladder, which signifies the steps of success, celebrating the time that has elapsed, while his future seems to be invisible to the eye. Reality exists somewhere beyond his sight. Cherishing the memories of his past life in his village and contrasting them with the present urban life, reflects his journey of life and how far he is from this timeline of the journey.


    Having lost some of the loveliest trees, gardens and forests, man now longs for those open fields, rich in wild grasses, regaled by the smell of pines and resonating with the soothing sound of bird songs. He craves for these awe-inspiring glimpses of rivers in a painting mood, laying down a lovely carpet of moss on its silty edges.

    Our great thinkers and artists talked of the habit of aesthetic apprehension to grasp the Beauty of Nature. It is in fact the ability to look beyond the level of isolated details and perceive the underlying cohesion in the Order of Nature.

    Human Art in particular has its primal impulse in the keen observance of Nature and our highly promising and talented artist, Veguri Ravindra Babu, living in a small village of Andhra Pradesh, grew up in the company of trees and birds, enjoying nature's majestic company, roaming around fields and forests and playing around enchanting streams and walking near ever flowing water of a nearby river. Alas, he saw the shrinking river, felt the pain of loss of friends - friends, yes those big shady trees, chirpy birds and colourful fishes. Bewildered and hurt, he expressed his anguish and gave vent to his feelings of betrayal in his lovely paintings.

    The world of Nature has a Pattern of Order which an artist or lover of Nature should try to manifest in his creative art. This artist is endowed with such high sense of apprehension and unique skill of expression that he is able to transmit it through his excellent works which portray those lovely patterns of beauty discovered in different shades of Nature - constructing harmonies in all the varied forms, in his captivating landscapes.


    # 533, Bird Song II, Acrylic & oil on canvas, 60 x 60 inches, 2009

    # 893, Perpetual Serenity, Oil on canvas, 48 x 48 inches, 2012

    His paintings and his installation "Disturbed Landscapes" convey a lofty message. His portrayal of gradual decline of human sensibilities to achieve hollow economic progress at the expense of our great Natural Order provokes us to ponder over our painful dilemma. He has bluntly portrayed victory of market forces over human values which blunted man's ability and sensibility to value both the services that natural systems provide to man and the aesthetics he enjoys by being in the Company of Nature.

    One is overtaken by a sense of awe and delight while viewing his masterly creations of Art which convey a deeper meaning that the world of Nature has a Pattern of Order. We need to see through the eyes of the artist those Sublime Shades of Nature which sadly we seem to have forgotten!


    # 536, Reminiscence II, Acrylic & oil on canvas, 60 x 84 inches, 2009


    H C Parikh

    Retired Director General of Income Tax, has been committed to the cause of Environmental Protection and Ecological Preservation. He writes extensively on all issues related to Environment and lectures in India and abroad on this issue as also on Indian Philosophy, Ethical aspect of Management, Education and Development and Empowerment of the needy, handicapped and dispossessed. He is the National President of 'Eye Care Foundation', an NGO working to enlighten people about the causes of blindness and semi-blindness. Also associated with many other NGOs and Service Organisations.
