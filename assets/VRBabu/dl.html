    •     Disturbed Landscapes:

    Artist's Thoughts

    This installation signifies the disturbances in the environment due to anthropogenic actions and the need to restore our natural beauty of village environment and regain a long term healthy ecosystem. It focuses on the changing face of my village environment and urbanization, the root cause of landscape disturbances eventually leading to species extinction and degeneration of society

    In this art work, the use of traditional coal filled hollow iron box depicts the earth's crust. The earth's crust is composed of a variety of rocks - the outer solid crust, underlain by a mantle and solid inner core. Much of our natural resources are deep seated in the earth's crust which we are indiscriminately using up for our benefits to meet up the demands of a growing population which is the principal source of degeneration of our environment. We must realize that nature is exhaustible. So, I think there is a need to replenish our natural resources and regain the beauty and health of our nature - the central idea of this artwork. I am intending to bring back the lost beauty of earth (village) and the desire to spread greenery everywhere. This is reflected in the hollow iron box filled with coal (a source of carbon), a pollutant which is the principal cause of disturbance to the environment. The use of the traditional iron box suggests our return to those simple, traditional ways that once helped preserve our ecology. Landscape disturbances need to be controlled to enable human habitation, to regain our greenery and lost beauty of our villages. On our march towards progress, with the help of technology, we have no right to destroy nature.


    Title: Disturbed Landscapes
    Size: 108.5 x 81 x 44 inches
    Medium: Iron and Wood
    Weight: 500 kgs - filled with soil
    Year of Creation: 2010
    In collaboration with Vadodara Sculpture Park

    In a symbolic suggestion that much of the earth should be under cultivation, I have sown natural seeds focusing on growth, in an effort to initiate a Green Revolution, and recover the lost beauty of earth. It signifies that the top cover of the earth's crust should be protected from deforestation and pollution thus increasing the crop yield by bringing more land under cultivation. I have used natural seeds sown in the soil - filled iron box in line with the natural eco-system.

    Illegal land acquisition has led to defacing of green lands; also uncontrolled urbanization and disturbed landscapes have created environmental and spatial disturbances. The bearing capacity of the earth is now significantly disproportionate to the demands placed on it by the growing population. The green fields that once enhanced the beauty of my village are no more. Most of the cultivable lands have been transformed into residential plots and mills or factories attracting large number of people to work, resulting in building of small towns in the last couple of decades. These towns have conjugated into cities leading to urbanization. The need to improve one's living standard, beyond basic sustenance, is the reason for such urbanization, which is not possible in small family farms, dependant on unpredictable environmental conditions. Most of the villagers are uninterested in agricultural practices. The natural vigour and vitality of the seed is not there, everything is now cultured in laboratories. The hybrids are booming now, there is no purity in the food we eat and the water we drink. The famous saying that "the grass is always greener on the other side" is true, as large flocks move to the cities to seek economic opportunities.

    In creating a concrete jungle, we have destroyed our natural surroundings. We have a ' do not care' attitude towards nature. Deforestation, industrial growth and effluents from chemical industries have resulted in degradation of our land and water resources on and under the surface. This has resulted in rivers drying up and marine fauna disappearing from lakes. With advancement in technology, smoke from motorized vehicles and half burnt coal cause pollution and temperature to rise due to CO emissions, 2 contributing to global warming which is dangerous for life. We, ourselves are the creators of these disturbances on landscape. Man's ecological footprint is too big to be sustained. The resources on earth have been exploited by human activities in the last couple of decades at an unimaginable rate. These human activities within the fluvial corridors have stressed nature, leading to loss of land cover and extinction of species.

                           

    Recently, going through a zoological article, I came to know that 25% of bird species have gone extinct and our national animal, the Tiger is also on the verge of extinction. We cannot find those chirping sparrows building their nests in creeks and crevices of our home. To enable animal habitation, deforestation should be strictly prohibited and we must consider long term ecosystem health. Afforestation programmes should be adopted in cities and villages, thus enriching greenery all around. This will reduce suffocation caused by pollution, purify the air we breathe and resolve problems of soil erosion. To make up for the follies, there is a need to restore nature and its resources. I think we should adopt a holistic view of nature - it is not an entity that exists separately from us, the nature is us, we are an inalienable part of it and we should care for it, in the most appropriate manner. Only then can we solve the problem of landscape and environmental disturbances.
