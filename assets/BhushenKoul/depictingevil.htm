Reconstruction of experiences


Depiciting evil in disguise
- The Statesman

Bhushen Koul’s paintings make one’s hair stand on end while its usage of bone-chilling archetypal imagery of evil - Bhushen’s inspired poetic metaphor depicts the travails and mortification of humanity and excruciatingly painful wrenching of it soul under the relentless charges of evil on life’s terminal co-terminal ends, i.e. the earth and the sky. It spreads its massive shapeless and irregular sable form (symbolizing chaos) on the sky and the earth to humiliate, circumscribe and even drive out light to enervate the inner strengths of divinity in man in order to degrade the true intent of life. And in that cursed moment of Fate when good under some mysterious inner law temporarily withdraws, evil simulates the act of transubstantiation to change---

Bhushen has been able to bring the widest scope to his personal predicament, privation, misery by raising it to the level of universal loss and therefore tragedy of immense magnitude. This as is well known, happens to poetic souls in all art forms. To disburden himself of a personal tragedy he had to understand and internalize its basic structure to handle it properly poetically. He had to internalize colour to colour it with the true content and quality of tragedy that had struck him so as to pour itself out in detached forms in order to secure them from petty particularization, lugubrious sentiment, maudlin emotion. What required was the constant application of severe controls on blending, choice and handling of colours. To have been able to open out his soul in such wonderful manner is a great tribute to him .

The sensitive artist has laid threadbare the heart lacerating experience of the tragedy that struck Kashmir------ Bhushen’s birth place. That he raises no accusing finger on any makes it more unbearable.
