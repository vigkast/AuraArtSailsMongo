Reconstruction of experiences


Artist's note

My art, in essence, is ‘experience endured and enlivened’. Feeling triggered off by experience and ensuring thought process being abstract by their nature, my creative effort concerns itself with constructing a recognizable visual parallel to indistinct features of what I must essay. I aim at transforming the abstract into comprehensible in a pictorial semantics which, besides building the desired aesthetic import must amply convey the message. The process I go through determines my pictorial vocabulary, the treatment I must employ, the colours I must bring into play, so as to make my work impassioned matching the experience that inspired it.
Lately the whole process has become significantly obvious. Having had the traumatic experience back in the valley, it is only natural that the same should find expression in my works. Exodus cannot obliterate association. The images and expression and experiences from the past in the Valley continues to occupy deep recesses of mind. They are persistent, overwhelming and force their way to ascertain their placement in my works. I don’t know if there is anything un duly autobiographical about my works in this series. The eminent art critic Shri Keshav Malik described them as – specimens of controlled purposeful art sending out urgent life signals. With their impact lying in their passionate message. The place has been userbed, yet the past lives with me, and it is unlimited.



My works after the exodus from the valley

My works are the reconstruction of inexplicably exuberant feelings, an experience enlivened and observed or one simple discomfort of an ecstasy-pang of remorse or simply the burden of experience and existence. My works are substantive, thematic in the literary sense, but authentically true to the medium. It is a journey of enriched experiential wisdom, the journey not ventured outwards as it exists within now.
The working space can be defined as an extended tapestry of dream. The figuration is not ectoplasm-like apparitions in a mindscape. They are figures of hope, despair, longing and being born out of the empathy. They have been prized out of my consciousness.
These are not images but happenings.

They are Real.
