Reconstruction of experiences


Bhushen’s recent canvasses
- The Times Of India

Bhushen Koul from Kashmir as he was educated at Baroda University’s Fine Arts faculty and like his senior ......., appears to have benefited from the palette of his guru, the great N.S. Bendre. But, like ........., Koul has never believed in imitation. Doing on independent lines is the finest tribute you can pay to a master.

Also, it is doubtful whether Bhushen had the direct benefit of Bendre’s teaching. Somehow an aura lingers behind and Koul’s paintings which look like they are bathed in some sort of celestial light must have at one time, been steeped in aura of the Bendre tradition.

Bhushen Koul is a painter who has obviously found a spiritual feeling in the world of senses. There are other worldly luminous areas in his canvas, and there are also images of human beings and serpents mysteriously clothed in wisps of light. Whatever he essays it is with a masterly touch.

He says of his own work “experience enlivened and observed – that is my art I reconstruct inexplicably exuberant feelings and look at them with pensive wonderment." This indeed, is the impact the viewer has. The colours are romantically sumptuous but never gaudy.

In fact a gentle touch is the hallmark of a Koul painting. Even those who would set out to unravel the significance of the image components will not press too hard but allow themselves to be bathed in translucent light and colour. Bhushen Koul is indeed a distinctive painter and one welcomes his visit here.
