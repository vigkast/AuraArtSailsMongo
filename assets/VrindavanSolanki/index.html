My Life's Journey

I am a child of a middle class, Gujarati family. I was nurtured by my mother’s faith and father’s belief in karma. The warmth and mystique of the mountain Girnar that rises above my hometown, Junagadh, contributed to my nourishment.

Friend like Chitra - my beloved wife, has been my silent witness and has provided me strong support. Today, she walks shoulder to shoulder with me on my creative journey. In fact, this journey commenced in 1968 or 69, when I was studying at the Faculty of Fine Arts at Baroda. My teacher, Mani Saheb, shared with me his thoughts on art and the creative process, which forever changed my inner self. Nostalgia for my hometown, the harshness and tenderness of village folk, as well as the range of their clothes; the rocks and trees of Girnar: all these were reflected in my work in the early days.

After this, there emerged images of old dwellings of Gujarat, its lanes and narrower bylanes, “pan shops”… Junagadh my hometown sprang to life, in its eloquent entirety, on white paper, through black ink. Those early works gave rise to my visual language which found its own corner in the world of art. I created my visual language through the medium of etchings, lithographs, linocuts, charcoal and blow-torch on wood. Though I draw lines of various hues even today, my preferred mediums now are acrylic and oil on canvas.

Even as I wander through both the national and international terrain of the art world, I am forever attracted to the soil of my native land - India, to its people, its sky, its water and air. It perpetually moves me, internally and externally.
I am bound by it.

My series of works like Mother and Child, My Land - My People, Houses of Gujarat, Stone series, Mumbai series, TATA Steel series, Faceless – Face series: all were crafted along this path.

Aura Art has graciously tried to give you glimpses of my long journey.

- Vrindavan Solanki
