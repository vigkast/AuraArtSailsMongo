“Mumbai Facebook 2012” by Prakash Bal Joshi / Amisha Mehta
"Mumbai Facebook 2012"
by Prakash Bal Joshi / Amisha Mehta
Mirror, screen, pedastal
72” x 48”
2012
Life in metropolis across the globe is really difficult despite all the facilities and amenities available. Life in Mumbai, described as melting pot of Indian culture is not any different where this is the concept for art installation where artists want to make viewers aware of identity loss due to urbanization process. IN a metropolis like Mumbai, people move in large numbers and become faceless in urban milieu.



“Infinite Blue Cosmos” by Sukant Panigrahy
"Infinite Blue Cosmos"
by Sukant Panigrahy
Plastic, water, metal, mirror, natural color
180” by 144” diameter
Cosmos.....the installation is a representation of the infinite blue that we all exist in. It invites the individual to consider the beauty of the inner & outer worlds and to inspire awareness of how our every action has an implication...that is mirrored in the larger cosmos...every drop of water wasted, energy used, every pollutant released has a profound effect on our universe.

“Buddha - The Nirakar” by Bhagwan Rampure
“Buddha - The Nirakar”
by Bhagwan Rampure
Appoxiresin with bronze effect
44" x 36" x 20"

Nirakar, the formless. God has no form, although all forms are his. This paradox becomes possible only because he has no form. If he had any particular form then he could not have all the forms. He is liquid, utterly ,liquid. Because he is formless. He can move into all forms, he can take on all forms, and yet he is not exhausted by the forms. He is inexhaustible energy.



“Dissolving Buddha” by Bhagwan Rampure
"Dissolving Buddha"
by Bhagwan Rampure
Appoxiresin with artificial marble effect
24" x 34" x 19"
The core of any spiritual Path is about to dissolve the “I” and then to rebuild it. At a certain Point of Time everything, including the “I”, will dissolve and vanish. In Buddhism it is taught that dissolving the Illusions of the Ego is the Path to Illumination. In Theravadin Buddhism, it is taught that the Ego itself dissolves when one attains Enlightenment.

“Silent Noise” by Saini S Johray
"Silent Noise"
by Saini S Johray
Fiber glass and metal
36” x 120” x 72”
The artist has primarily expressed the ill-consequences of Noise Pollution. He stresses on the vulnerability of one of our most precious gifts of nature, the sense of Hearing and the havoc caused by the outrageous decibel levels in any given Urban setting. This includes blaring traffic horns, deafening music (discotheques, headphones) and insane firecrackers. These are all unnatural conditions which most probably the human body was never built to sustain.
