Dhruva Misrty

Artist Profile

Dhruva Mistry was born in 1957 in Gujarat in India and studied Sculpture at the Faculty of Fine Arts, MS University of Baroda from 1974-1981 and at the Royal College of Art in London on a British Council Scholarship from 1981-1983. He was Artist in Residence in association with the Arts Council at Kettle’s Yard Gallery in Cambridge with a Fellowship at the Churchill College, University of Cambridge in 1984-85. Dhruva represented Britain at the Third Rodin Grand Prize Exhibition, Japan in 1990 and was Elected Member at the Royal Academy of Arts in 1991. Birmingham City Council appointed him principal artist for the Victoria Square Sculptures in 1992-1993. He was elected Fellow of the Royal Society of British Sculptors, London in 1993. Selected by the Asian Art Museum, Fukuoka, to represent India for the Asian Artist Today - Fukuoka Annual VII Exhibition in 1994. In 1997, he resumed his work in Vadodara, India. Mistry was awarded Honorary CBE in 2001. Dhruva has held over 30 solo exhibitions apart from being included in significant national and international shows since 1979 and has done some major site specific works and public commissions. His works are held in important public and private collections in the UK, Japan and India, including the Tate, Arts Council, Royal Academy, Victoria & Albert Museum, Royal College of Art in London, Hunterian Art Gallery, Glasgow the National Museum of Wales, Sculpture at Goodwood, Yorkshire Sculpture Park in the UK and Fukuoka Asian Art Museum in Japan, University of Delhi, Lalit Kala Akademi in New Delhi and Roopankar Museum in Bhopal.



Arzan Khambatta




Artist Profile

Arzan Khambatta calls himself an architect by profession and a sculptor by choice.  Encouraged by his parents, he started sculpting the very next day after giving his 10th standard exams. He would scout the streets, garages, construction sites for scrap metal, collect the same and weld them into pieces of art he fondly christened “SCRAPTURES”.  A word that has become synonymous with Arzan.  His works have been displayed in 10 solo shows and over 50 group shows around the country and the globe.

Gourishankar Soni - Dor
       

Painted Charkhies - Dor Project by Gaurishankar Soni,
4 feet and 1.5 feet length, 2013

Thoughts of the Artist

My installation has a group of spindles … in the kite festival there are many strings from spindles soaring in the sky… many big and small spindles play their respective roles … representing people. The strings spun around the spindle are a medium of spreading our words, emotions/feelings, dreams, happiness … to the world. Along with the soaring kites the pattern of the strings on the spindle also soar into the sky as if merging its fragrance with the Universe. And when we wind the string back onto the spindle then it is just the string and no pattern but just an image of the pattern, which never can be retrieved no matter how much we desire. The form pattern changes to a formless imagery. The strings pattern on the spindle influences the thought and lifestyles of people whose fragrance is widespread in our culture and society. I want to share this experience of mine. Creating works of Art out of “Charkhi” or spindles, combining painting with sculpture, has been a very unique, fulfilling and interesting experience for me.

a mother, a wife, a friend, a stranger

Ratilal Kansodaria
   


Artist Profile

Born in 1961 in Gujarat. He completed B.A. (Fine), Sculpture and M.A. (Fine), Sculpture (Creative stream) in the years 1987 & 1990 respectively from the Faculty of Fine Arts, M.S. University, Baroda. A sculptor par excellence he has extensively displayed his sculptures at various solo exhibitions and group shows across the globe. A highly decorated artist with over a score awards and honours conferred upon him by various cultural institutions the most recent one being the Gaurav Puraskar Award by Gujarat State Lalit Kala Academy in 2009. Currently the Principal Incharge of C N College of Fine Arts, Ahmedabad.
